A Bit About Me

I am a city planner who possesses a deep conviction that deliberate and thoughtful urban solutions can effect meaningful change in our living environment. Demonstrating my commitment to this belief, I established Kainak, a distinguished design studio, with the purpose of fostering collaboration among the public, students, and organisations in order to develop innovative solutions that enhance urban life.


I successfully completed my undergraduate studies in Istanbul and furthered my academic journey by pursuing a Master's degree at the University College London (UCL) in the field of Urban Development.


I firmly hold the belief that through collective cooperation, systemic design thinking, and the implementation of inclusive policies, we have the capacity to address societal challenges and contribute to the betterment of our world.

Designing a circular development strategy for the metro-rural area of Istanbul

Nestled on Istanbul's periphery, Catalca thrives as an enclave of nearly 75,000 inhabitants, offering the city a unique blend of natural endowments and vibrant agricultural endeavors. A hallmark of distinction in my journey towards an urban and regional planning degree, my culmination project was dedicated to crafting an intricate circular development strategy tailored to this locale.

Read more/less

The strategic orchestration commenced with a comprehensive analysis and synthesis undertaken on a 1:100,000 scale, which then seamlessly transitioned into the formulation of an overarching regional strategy. This strategic blueprinting was further extended to encompass meticulously designed micro-level initiatives, poised to facilitate the pragmatic implementation of the proposed strategies within Catalca's central district and the serene Karacakoy Village. My purview encompassed the meticulous creation of master plans, technical implementation blueprints, and urban design propositions, carefully tailored to the area's unique attributes.
With the triumphant completion of this endeavor, I garnered the highest accolades, securing a place of honor in the dean's distinguished high honour list—an affirmation of my dedication to scholarly excellence.

Generating the design for zero-carbon eco-village, Lavia

Guided by the ethos of ecological harmony, our visionary project crystallized into an exquisite design for a zero-carbon eco-village, affectionately named Lavia. In a resounding triumph, our endeavor clinched the esteemed 1st prize in the Sustainability Competition, orchestrated by the Turkish Green Building Council.

Read more/less

Central to our innovation was the emblematic herb lavender, serving as both muse and model. Drawing inspiration from lavender's unique carbon-sequestering properties, we harnessed its innate capacity to bolster carbon retention within the soil. Beyond its ecological contributions, we conceived of lavender as an emblematic brand capable of fostering a vibrant tourist allure through its versatile applications, ease of cultivation, and distinctive characteristics.
At the heart of this endeavor was the ambition to forge a self-sustaining haven—one intricately woven into the fabric of nature, an embodiment of sustainability principles, and a beacon of carbon-neutrality. As the torchbearers of this transformative vision, our collective prowess secured the coveted top spot in the Sustainability Idea awards. My role, as the team leader, entailed meticulously devising a robust and viable strategy for the village's realization, alongside architecting an array of ingenious carbon-neutralization initiatives.

Forging a Path to Sustainable Rural Progress: Orchestrating a Regional Blueprint for Ödemiş

Within the embrace of Ödemiş lies a resplendent landscape, characterized by its agricultural abundance and nurturing livestock husbandry—a haven of modest population and a thriving rural essence. Propelled by this distinctive essence, our endeavor secured the distinguished 3rd prize in the "Cities Living with Rural Values National Student Idea Project Competition," proudly hosted by the Living Cities Platform.

Read more/less

The underpinning theme was "cluster," a concept that unveiled the shared threads weaving through Ödemiş's tapestry of villages. These connections were deftly woven to align with the villages' potential and aspirations, paving the way for a collective and cohesive pursuit of sustainable rural prosperity. Central to our vision was the empowerment of villages to collectively advance towards a harmonious and enduring rural lifestyle.
As the project leader, I orchestrated a symphony of strategic thought, crafting a robust roadmap for agricultural progression within the region. My role extended beyond the strategic realm to encompass the meticulous visualization of posters and tables, breathing life into our comprehensive endeavor.
